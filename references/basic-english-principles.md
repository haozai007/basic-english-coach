# Basic English Principles

Use these principles when teaching, rewriting, or translating.

## Core Idea

C.K. Ogden's Basic English aims to express many ideas with a small, controlled vocabulary and simple sentence structures. For this skill, the practical goal is not literary elegance. The goal is clear, learnable, usable English.

## Teaching Principles

- Teach with a small number of high-use words.
- Use short sentences before long sentences.
- Prefer concrete examples.
- Reuse the same patterns many times.
- Translate meaning, not word order.
- Explain in Chinese when it helps the learner notice a pattern.

## Expression Principles

- Prefer common operators: be, have, do, make, get, give, take, put, come, go, keep, let, see, say, seem, send, may, will.
- Prefer noun + simple verb structures.
- Break one complex Chinese sentence into two or three English sentences when needed.
- Use prepositions and particles carefully: in, on, at, to, from, with, for, by, about, before, after, over, under, through.
- Avoid long noun chains when a simple clause is clearer.

## Strictness

The assistant should say "Basic-English-like" when the response follows the spirit of Basic English but may include modern or non-core words. Use "natural simple English" for practical workplace language that may go beyond the original Basic English list.

## Common Transformations

- "confirm" -> "make certain" or "see if"
- "accept" -> "say yes to" or "take"
- "requirement" -> "need" or "thing needed"
- "investigate" -> "look into"
- "implement" -> "make" or "put into use"
- "postpone" -> "put off"
- "resolve" -> "put right" or "make right"
- "communicate" -> "say", "send word", or "talk with"
